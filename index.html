THE TERRESTRIAL CARBON CYCLE

Carbon moves constantly between the atmosphere, living organisms, soil, and human activity. This simulation models the biosphere's carbon cycle: photosynthesis pulls CO₂ from the air; respiration, decomposition, wildfires, and fossil-fuel burning return it. Use the controls to observe how each process shifts the atmospheric carbon balance.
